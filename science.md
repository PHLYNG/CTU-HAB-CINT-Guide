# The Science - What Are We Measuring?

Remote sensing is the process of detecting and monitoring the physical characteristics of a chosen subject that is far away from the person studying the subject.

For example, a camera on a satellite can take a picture of the Earth's surface.

In the case of this project, we will be using sensors to capture temperature, pressure, and humidity data all the way up to 100,000 feet above the surface.

While we are not discovering anything new nor are we using cutting edge technology, we will be able to build a payload that can demonstrate things we already collectively know using relatively inexpensive yet high performing hardware.
